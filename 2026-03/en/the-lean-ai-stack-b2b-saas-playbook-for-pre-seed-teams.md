# The Lean AI Stack: B2B SaaS Playbook for Pre-Seed Teams

## Enterprise Problem Statement
Modern enterprises are drowning in unstructured data and manual workflows. Legacy systems lack the agility to integrate generative AI, leading to significant operational bottlenecks. Teams spend 40% of their workweek on repetitive data entry, compliance checking, and report generation. This inefficiency results in delayed decision-making and inflated operational costs. For pre-seed AI founders, the opportunity lies not in building another model, but in solving the "last mile" problem of AI integration into existing enterprise workflows without disrupting security protocols.

## Solution with ROI Metrics
Our solution provides an API-first AI infrastructure layer that sits securely on top of legacy systems. By automating document processing and customer support triage, we deliver measurable value immediately. The ROI is calculated based on time saved per employee. For a typical medium business, implementing this stack reduces manual processing time by 60%. If an employee costs $50/hour and saves 10 hours a week, the annual savings per seat is $26,000. With a team of 20, the total annual ROI exceeds $500,000, justifying the software investment within the first quarter.

## Target: Enterprise/Medium Business/SMB
While the long-term vision targets Enterprise contracts, the initial beachhead focuses on Medium Businesses and tech-forward SMBs. These segments have shorter sales cycles and less bureaucratic procurement processes, allowing for faster iteration. Enterprise clients are targeted once security compliance is fully established. SMBs serve as the testing ground for product-market fit, while Medium Businesses provide the revenue stability needed to fund enterprise-grade security certifications.

## Enterprise Pricing ($999/$4999/month or seat-based)
Pricing must reflect value while accommodating different growth stages. The "Growth" tier is priced at $999/month, including up to 10 seats and standard API rate limits. This targets medium businesses ready to scale. The "Enterprise" tier is priced at $4999/month, offering unlimited seats, dedicated infrastructure, and priority support. Seat-based pricing is available for organizations needing granular control, set at $150/seat/month with a minimum of 10 seats. A free tier exists for individual developers to encourage viral adoption and community feedback.

## Sales Motion (PLG vs SL)
A hybrid motion is critical for emerging markets. Product-Led Growth (PLG) drives initial adoption through the free tier and self-serve onboarding. This allows indie hackers and small teams to integrate the tool without friction. Once usage metrics indicate expansion potential, the motion shifts to Sales-Led (SL). Account Executives engage high-usage accounts to negotiate Enterprise tiers. This bottom-up approach reduces customer acquisition cost (CAC) while ensuring high-value contracts are handled with white-glove service.

## Integration Requirements (SSO, SAML, API)
Enterprise readiness requires seamless integration. Single Sign-On (SSO) via SAML 2.0 is mandatory for the Enterprise tier to ensure identity management aligns with corporate IT policies. A robust RESTful API must be available for all paid tiers to allow custom workflow automation. Webhooks are essential for real-time data synchronization. For medium businesses, pre-built integrations with Slack, Salesforce, and HubSpot reduce time-to-value. Documentation must be developer-friendly to support the indie hacker community.

## Security & Compliance (SOC2, GDPR)
Trust is the currency of B2B AI. To sell to enterprises, SOC2 Type II compliance is non-negotiable. This audit validates security controls around data encryption, access management, and risk assessment. GDPR compliance is required for any customer data originating from the EU, ensuring right-to-be-forgotten and data portability. Pre-seed teams should budget for compliance early; using compliant cloud infrastructure providers can accelerate this process. Security documentation should be publicly available to reduce procurement friction.

## Enterprise Go-to-Market
The GTM strategy leverages community-driven growth to build awareness in an emerging market. Content marketing focusing on AI education establishes thought leadership. Partnerships with incubators and developer communities create viral loops.
